Heritage & Traditions

Traditional Architecture

From ancient wind towers (Barjeel) and coral stone walls to the majestic modern skyline, Dubai's architecture reflects its Bedouin heritage while embracing the future. Modern structures prioritize cutting-edge design and sustainability, representing global ambition.

Art & Calligraphy

Arabic calligraphy is the fundamental spiritual art form, adorning everything from mosques to modern buildings. This tradition now exists alongside a vibrant contemporary art scene in hubs like Alserkal Avenue, making Dubai a magnet for global artistic expression.

Cuisine & Hospitality

Traditional Emirati cuisine emphasizes hospitality (Fahala) with dishes like dates and slow-cooked meats. However, with over 200 nationalities present, Dubai is now a global gastronomic hub, offering a unique fusion of international street food and Michelin-starred dining.

Traditional Attire

Traditional dress, the Kandura (men) and Abaya (women), symbolizes local identity and modesty, reflecting religious and cultural values. This identity coexists with a dynamic global fashion scene, where diverse expats express a unique spectrum of personal styles.

Music & Dance

Al-Ayyala, a traditional group dance, celebrates victory and pride. The rhythm of drums and poetry tell stories of heritage and valor passed through generations.

Festivals & Celebrations

Eid Al-Fitr, Eid Al-Adha, and UAE National Day bring communities together through prayers, feasts, and spectacular public celebrations with fireworks and cultural performances.
